Auth lets you **integrate web3 flows into any backend setup or web2 service.**

More specifically, Auth makes use of the [Sign-in with Ethereum](https://eips.ethereum.org/EIPS/eip-4361) standard to enable:

- Securely sending the connected client-side wallet address to any backend
- Standard authentication flows for wallet-based login

### What can you do with Auth?

Auth enables all use cases that require user wallet addresses on the backend. These include:

- **Authentication:** Allow users to login to your backends and services with just their wallet
- **Subscriptions & Payments:** Allow users to make subscriptions and payments tied to their on-chain identity
- **Verify On-chain Identities:** Verify the on-chain identities of your existing users to unlock web3 experiences
- **Token Gating:** Securely gate content and only give access to users with necessary access (like token ownership)
